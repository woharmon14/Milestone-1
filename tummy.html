CASA VERDE: Gastronomic Experiences

The first time I heard of Casa Verde was during my trip to Cebu last year when I was looking for popular and recommended restaurants there. I was about to dine at their Cebu I.T. Park branch but had to change my plans for another dining option. Casa Verde was established in Cebu in 2002 by a family with Spanish roots, with its original branch starting as a small canteen at the family’s ancestral home. 12 years and three branches later, Casa Verde has become one of the most popular and well-loved restaurants in Cebu.

Even if I was not able to try Casa Verde in Cebu, I was so delighted when I found out that they had opened a branch at the second floor of the U.P. Town Center. So now Metro Manila can have a taste of what made Casa Verde so popular in Cebu. The branch here is not that big, probably seating less than 80 people, so the lines can get very very long during peak dining hours. Expect to wait 30 minutes to one hour during weekends. It seems that their popularity has already spread here.

Casa Verde’s menu is quite simple and straightforward. It serves modern day comfort food items like burgers, pasta, ribs, and steaks with several mouthwatering desserts. One thing to take note of at Casa Verde, despite its affordable prices, the serving sizes of its dishes are very generous. You can even say that they are huge! It’s enough for one hungry diner or for two not-so-hungry persons. This is in line with the restaurant’s philisophy of “Value for Money.”

Caryl’s Country Salad (P193) is the kind of salad that I would like especially with its breaded chicken strips topped on a bed of crisp greens, diced eggs, shredded cheese, bacon, and tomatoes with honey mustard dressing.

Some other items which my friends have tried include The Exculiber (P199) which is two burgers in one. It comes with two beef patties, mayo, lettuce, tomatoes, onions and cheese on a sesame seed bun. The burger itself is actually not that extraordinary but I do like their presentation with a knife plunged squarely in the middle of the burger.

While the steak was quite good, unfortunately for foodie friend, this was probably the only dish that had a small serving, relative to their other bigger-sized dishes. It occupied only one fourth of the plate and I could see Foodie friend salivating over my Surf & Turf dish which seemed twice as big. It did not come with rice, so foodie friend ended up a bit “bitin” over his lunch. He vowed to come back another day and have his revenge by getting Brian’s Ribs instead.

Casa verde in my tummy. lol! Burp! People just can’t get enough of its hefty servings and pocket-friendly prices. This is my most frequented restaurant at U.P. Town Center and I can’t wait to go back for more.

You can definitely see why Casa Verde has been able to create its own enormous following here in Manila. Hopefully they expand their branches in Manila even further but this is one restaurant I am willing to wait in line for.
